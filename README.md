## 🌑 The Beginning

ERPB was never planned as a “project.”

It didn’t begin in a classroom assignment, a hackathon briefing, or a technical requirement document.

It began much earlier; in observation.

Late nights, endless scrolling, and watching K-Dramas like *“Teach You a Lesson”* exposed a recurring theme:

- Institutions with unchecked authority  
- Students without structured voice  
- Complaints that disappear without trace  
- Systems that respond only when they choose to  

At first, it was just content.

Then it stopped feeling like fiction.

It started feeling like a pattern.

And that’s where the idea of ERPB was born.

---

## ⚖️ What ERPB Represents

ERPB (Education Rights & Protection Bureau) is an **independent digital awareness and reporting system** designed to structure educational grievances into traceable, observable data.

It is not a government body.  
It is not a legal authority.  
It does not issue judgments.

Instead, it serves one purpose:

> To ensure that incidents in educational spaces are not forgotten, diluted, or ignored.

---

## 🧭 The Core Philosophy

Traditional complaint systems often operate in isolation:
a form is submitted → a system processes it → and the outcome disappears behind closed doors.

ERPB challenges that model.

It introduces visibility.

Every report becomes part of a structured ecosystem:

- Reports are no longer isolated incidents  
- They become patterns  
- Patterns become signals  
- Signals become awareness  

ERPB is built on a simple principle:

> **Individual voices matter less in isolation — but become powerful in structure.**

## 🧩 System Architecture Overview

ERPB is structured into interconnected layers:

### 1. Incident Reporting Layer
A structured intake system where users can:
- Submit incident reports
- Provide institutional context
- Attach severity levels
- Optionally remain anonymous
- Add supporting evidence links

---

### 2. Authentication Layer
A secure user identity system ensures:
- Only verified users can submit reports
- Spam and misuse are reduced
- Accountability exists where required

Anonymity is preserved when necessary for safety.

---

### 3. Public Transparency Layer (Reports)
A live system displaying submitted incidents in a structured format:
- Chronological feed of reports
- Categorized incidents
- Institutional context visibility
- Anonymous identity masking when enabled

---

### 4. Watchlist Intelligence Layer
One of the core conceptual modules of ERPB.

Instead of treating each report independently, ERPB groups and analyzes data:

- Repeated institutions are identified  
- Severity trends are evaluated  
- Risk levels are assigned  
- Institutions are surfaced based on pattern frequency  

This transforms ERPB from a reporting tool into a **pattern recognition system**.

---

## 📊 Watchlist Logic (Conceptual Model)

Institutions are evaluated based on:

- Frequency of complaints  
- Severity distribution  
- Recurrence patterns over time  

Each institution is classified into:

- Low Risk  
- Medium Risk  
- High Risk  
- Critical Attention Zone  

> The goal is not labeling.  
> The goal is awareness through aggregation.

---

## 🔐 Privacy & Responsibility Model

ERPB operates under strict boundaries:

- It does not verify claims as legal truth  
- It does not act as a judicial authority  
- It does not publish private identities publicly  

Instead, it balances:

- Transparency  
- Anonymity  
- Accountability  

Users are responsible for the accuracy of submitted information.
